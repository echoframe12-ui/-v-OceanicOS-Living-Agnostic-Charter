# OceanicOS Constitution

This constitution defines the operating principles for OceanicOS as an open, living, and agnostic platform foundation.

## Article I — Purpose

OceanicOS exists to help people and organizations coordinate work through transparent, explainable, and interoperable systems. The charter is not a rigid rulebook; it is a shared basis for decision-making and implementation.

## Article II — Core Principles

1. Openness
   - Make decisions, processes, and knowledge legible to others.
2. Interoperability
   - Favor systems that work across tools, communities, and models.
3. Human agency
   - Keep people, consent, and accountability at the center of design.
4. Resilience
   - Build for continuity, recovery, and change.
5. Evidence over hype
   - Prefer documented reasoning, visible outcomes, and measurable progress.

## Article III — Operating Rules

- All significant changes must be proposed, discussed, and recorded.
- Planning must be explicit, explainable, and reviewable.
- Memory must preserve context without hiding its sources or assumptions.
- Tool access must be auditable and bounded by clear consent.
- The platform must support multiple models and open integrations rather than locking users into one vendor.

## Article IV — Governance

- Contributors propose changes through issues or pull requests.
- Maintainers review proposals for alignment with the constitution.
- Major changes should be captured in a decision record.
- Disagreements should be resolved through discussion, evidence, and amendment rather than force.

## Article V — Amendment

This constitution may be amended by consensus of maintainers and contributors through a documented review process. Any amendment should preserve the living, agnostic, and human-centered intent of the project.
