# Layer 3: Core Engines

The Core Engines are the internal drivers of the OceanicOS system, providing the fundamental logic for interacting with reality, managing knowledge, and making decisions.

## Engine Definitions

### 1. Reality Engine
Interfaces with the physical and digital world to gather evidence and observe context. It is the primary sensor for Layer 0 (Reality).

### 2. Memory & Knowledge Engines
Responsible for the persistent storage of experiences (Memory) and the semantic organization of information (Knowledge). They support the Human Kernel (Layer 2).

### 3. Verification & Decision Engines
The logical core that ensures truth (Verification) and determines the best course of action (Decision) based on evidence and principles.

### 4. Planning & Simulation Engines
Enable the system to look ahead, design complex workflows (Planning), and test outcomes in a safe environment (Simulation).

### 5. Builder Engine
The engine that drives the creation and iteration of the system itself, following the Universal Builder Cycle.

### 6. Registry & Security Engines
Maintain the official record of capabilities (Registry) and ensure the integrity and safety of the entire OS (Security).
